Suicidal behavior is a major health concern in many countries, developed and developing alike. At least a million people are estimated to die annually from suicide worldwide. Many more people, especially the young and middle-aged, attempt suicide. In the past few decades, although suicide rates have been reported to be stable or declining in many developed countries, an upward trend has been observed in youth suicide rates. These data reflect the impact of mental illness on teenagers which leads to suicide.

Depression in the working population is also complex, and its causes have not been well understood. However, it is recognized that risk factors unrelated to work play a role, such as a workplace impact of excessive work stress on employees and the impact of depression on employees in the workplace. Evidence is accumulating that links work organizations to depression and other mental health problems, as well as increased productivity losses. Multiple studies of various occupations have identified several sources of work stress that may be associated with depression (e.g., high work requirements, low work control, lack of social support in the workplace).
