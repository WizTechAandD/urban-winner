Chronic Conditions by Sleep Duration

People who get less than 7 hours of sleep are more likely to report chronic health conditions such as stroke, depression, cancer, etc. This tells us that sleep is one of the necessary components of recovery and healing. If one does not get good quality sleep, chronic health conditions will arise as the body isn’t recovered properly and this leads to over stressing the body.

Short Sleep Duration Among HS Students

With highschool students, around two thirds report having less than 8 hours per night. Students with insufficient sleep increase as they pass a grade. Short sleep duration is lowest among grade 9 students and highest among grade 12 students. This could suggest that as students pass a grade the subject matter increases in difficulty and the course loads increase, especially with added pressure to choose courses that will be needed for a college or university that the students aren’t completely decided on.

Health Risk Factors by Sleep Duration

Short sleepers were linked to a higher risk in obesity, smoking more, and a higher chance of consuming alcohol in excess than people with sufficient sleep. This could mean that not getting enough sleep could lead people to worsen with addictions which diminishes their health. Not getting sufficient sleep will make it harder to quit any addictions one may have.
